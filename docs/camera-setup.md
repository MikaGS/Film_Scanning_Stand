# Camera Setup

This document describes the camera setup used with the modular film scanning stand.

The stand is designed for camera-based film digitization of 35mm and medium format negatives. The system can be adapted to different cameras, lenses and film formats.

## Camera

For this build, a mirrorless camera is used for digitizing the negatives.

The camera is mounted to the camera carrier using a leveling plate, an optional panorama head and an optional Arca-Swiss quick release plate.

## Lens

A macro lens is recommended for film scanning.

The lens should be sharp, flat-field if possible, and able to focus close enough for the required reproduction ratio.

## Mounting Stack

Typical mounting order:

1. Camera carrier
2. Camera leveling plate
3. Optional panorama head
4. Optional Arca-Swiss quick release clamp
5. Camera

The leveling plate is used to align the camera parallel to the film plane.

The panorama head can be useful for fine rotational adjustment.

The Arca-Swiss quick release clamp makes it easier to mount and remove the camera without changing the main setup.

## Camera Settings

Recommended starting settings:

| Setting | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| File format | RAW |
| ISO | Base ISO |
| Aperture | f/5.6 to f/8 |
| Focus | Manual focus |
| White balance | Fixed manual white balance |
| Stabilization | Off when mounted rigidly |
| Shutter | Self-timer, remote release or electronic shutter |
| Exposure | Manual |

Avoid auto exposure and auto white balance, since they can create inconsistencies between scans.

## Focus

Focus manually on the film grain.

Use live view magnification if available.

After focusing, avoid touching the lens or camera unless the setup is recalibrated.

## Light Source

Use a stable, diffused and even light source.

The light should cover the full film area without visible hotspots or color shifts.

For color negative film, a high-quality light source with good color rendering is recommended.

## Scanning Workflow

1. Clean the film.
2. Place the film in the holder.
3. Turn on the light source and let it stabilize.
4. Set camera exposure manually.
5. Focus on the film grain.
6. Capture the scan.
7. For larger formats, capture overlapping images for stitching.
8. Export or process the RAW files in the preferred software.

## Notes

Keep the camera, lens, film holder and light source fixed during a scanning session.

For stitched scans, do not change exposure, focus or white balance between frames.
