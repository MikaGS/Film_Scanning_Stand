# Build Guide

This guide describes the assembly process of the modular film scanning stand.

## 1. Print All Parts

Start by 3D printing all required parts for the frame adapters, top bearing mount, bottom support, camera carrier, servo mount and optional accessories.

After printing, remove all support material and clean the screw holes if necessary.

## 2. Install Heat-Set Inserts

Install the required heat-set inserts into the printed parts.

Use:

- M3 inserts for smaller mechanical connections and the linear rail mounting points
- M4 inserts where medium-strength connections are required
- M5 inserts for the adapters that connect to the aluminium extrusion

Make sure the inserts sit straight and flush with the printed surface.

## 3. Assemble the Aluminium Extrusion and Linear Rail

Attach the linear rail to the aluminium extrusion using the 3D-printed rail adapters.

The rail is mounted to the adapters with M3 screws.  
The adapters are mounted to the aluminium extrusion with M5 screws and slot nuts / T-nuts.

### Steps

1. Insert the M5 slot nuts into the aluminium extrusion.
2. Position the 3D-printed adapters on the extrusion.
3. Loosely attach the adapters with M5 screws.
4. Place the linear rail onto the adapters.
5. Attach the rail to the adapters using M3 screws.
6. Align the rail parallel to the aluminium extrusion.
7. Tighten all screws gradually.
8. Move the linear rail carriage through the full travel distance to check for smooth movement.

If the carriage binds or feels rough, loosen the screws slightly, realign the rail and tighten again.

## 4. Assemble the Top Bearing Part

Insert the bearing into the top printed part.

After the bearing is seated correctly, screw on the lid to hold the bearing in place.

Then attach the complete top bearing assembly to the aluminium extrusion.

## 5. Assemble the Bottom Part

Assemble the bottom printed part and attach it to the aluminium extrusion.

This part is designed as a press-fit connection. Carefully press it onto the extrusion until it is fully seated.

Make sure it sits straight, since it defines the lower support point for the lead screw.

## 6. Prepare the Camera Carrier

Install the nuts for the lead screw into the first half of the camera carrier they should be able to move.

Make sure the nuts are aligned correctly so that the lead screw can rotate smoothly without binding.

## 7. Attach the Camera Carrier to the Linear Rail

Attach the first half of the camera carrier to the linear rail carriages using eight M3 screws.

Tighten the screws evenly so the carrier sits flat against the rail carriages.

Check that the carrier can move smoothly along the rail.

## 8. Add Grease

Add a small amount of grease into the dedicated grease compartment.

This helps lubricate the lead screw and improves the smoothness of the height adjustment.

Do not overfill the compartment.

## 9. Attach the Carrier Grease compartment Lid

Attach the lid to the first half of the camera carrier.

## 10. Mount the Camera Leveling Plate

Mount the camera leveling plate to the second half of the camera carrier.

This plate will be used to fine-adjust the camera alignment during scanning.

Make sure it is securely attached, since camera stability is important for sharp scans and accurate stitching.

## 11. Join the Camera Carrier Halves

Attach the second half of the camera carrier to the first half.

Check that all screw holes align correctly and tighten the screws evenly.

The camera carrier should now be rigid, but still move freely along the linear rail.

## 12. Install the Lead Screw

Screw the lead screw through the top bearing, the camera carrier nuts and into the bottom support.

Rotate it slowly by hand to make sure it runs smoothly.

If there is resistance, check the alignment of the top bearing, bottom support and lead screw nuts.

## 13. Attach the Stepper Motor

Attach the stepper motor to the printed motor mount.

Connect the stepper motor to the lead screw using the coupling or drive mechanism, depending on the final design.

Make sure the motor shaft is aligned with the lead screw. Misalignment can cause binding, increased wear or uneven movement.

## 14. Optional: Short the Stepper Motor Leads

To increase passive holding resistance when the motor is not powered, the stepper motor phases can be shorted together.

This makes the motor harder to turn by hand and helps prevent the camera carrier from moving unintentionally.

Only short the stepper motor leads when the motor driver is disconnected or when the driver circuit is designed for this use.

Do not short the outputs of a powered stepper driver.

## 15. Attach the Stand to the Base

### Add Feet to the Base

Attach the feet to the base plate to improve stability and to protect the work surface.

### Attach the Stand to the Base

Place the assembled vertical stand onto the base plate and align it carefully.

Make sure the aluminium extrusion is perpendicular to the base and positioned so the camera carrier can move freely without hitting the base or light source.

### Steps

1. Position the assembled stand on the base plate.
2. Mark the mounting hole positions.
3. Drill pilot holes into the base plate.
4. Align the stand with the drilled holes.
5. Screw the stand securely onto the base plate.
6. Check that the vertical extrusion is straight and does not wobble.
7. Verify that the camera carrier can move through the full travel range.

If necessary, use a square or angle gauge to check that the vertical stand is mounted at 90° to the base.

## 16. Add Optional Parts

Optional accessories can be added depending on the scanning workflow and required adjustment precision.

### Panorama Head

A panorama head can be mounted between the camera carrier and the camera leveling plate.

This allows finer rotational adjustment and makes it easier to align the camera perfectly parallel to the film plane. It is especially useful for stitched scans of larger formats such as 6x7, 6x9 or 6x12.

### Arca-Swiss Quick Release Plate

An Arca-Swiss quick release plate can be added for faster camera mounting and removal.

This makes it easier to switch cameras, remove the camera for other use, or reposition the camera without fully disassembling the scanning setup.

Make sure the quick release plate is mounted securely and does not introduce flex or unwanted movement.

### Optional Laser-Cut Pieces

If used, add the optional laser-cut pieces at this stage.

These can be used for additional stiffness, covers, alignment aids, or visual finishing parts.

Make sure they do not interfere with the movement of the camera carrier or the lead screw.

## Final Check

Before mounting the camera, check the full mechanical movement:

- The carrier moves smoothly along the full rail travel
- The lead screw turns without binding
- The stepper is properly aligned
- The aluminium extrusion and rail are rigidly connected
- The camera leveling plate is secure
- All screws are tightened
- No printed part is under unwanted stress

The stand is now ready for camera mounting and calibration.
